# Hiring & Succeeding With Your 1st Head of Sales

- https://share.fireflies.ai/embed/meetings/qTSktmzNrShe3NjP

Welcome and Introduction (00:02 - 01:33)
Esteban welcomes attendees and expresses excitement about Armando's return to share his expertise. Armando has extensive experience in building go-to-market (GTM) teams, having worked at companies like Google, Dropbox, and Salesforce.

Hiring the First Salesperson (01:40 - 12:44)
Armando discusses the challenges of hiring the first salesperson, emphasizing the need for scrappy, self-directed individuals (referred to as "Jane") who can help build processes. He contrasts this with later-stage hires ("Jack"), who should be more process-oriented and experienced. Key characteristics of early-stage sales hires include being evangelistic, creating their own materials, and valuing access and praise over compensation.

Motivations of Salespeople (08:21 - 10:16)
The primary motivation for salespeople is job security, followed by the desire for career progression and recognition. Armando emphasizes that salespeople care about their performance metrics as a reflection of their capabilities.

Finding the Right Candidates (11:00 - 13:43)
Armando suggests looking for newly minted Account Executives (AEs) or business development representatives (BDRs) from reputable companies, failed entrepreneurs, or individuals with business acumen from private equity or venture capital backgrounds. He advises against hiring based solely on industry knowledge, especially for early-stage roles.

Sales Process and Structure (14:01 - 19:59)
The conversation shifts to the importance of understanding the sales process and the need for salespeople to be adaptable. Armando discusses the difference between hiring for early-stage versus later-stage sales roles, stressing the importance of industry knowledge for sales leaders.

Onboarding and Expectations (22:36 - 43:01)
Armando highlights the unrealistic expectations founders often have for new sales hires regarding autonomy and performance. He stresses the importance of ongoing support from founders during the sales process and the need for clear communication and collaboration.

Organizing Sales Teams (43:34 - 49:08)
Armando proposes a "SWAT team" approach for organizing sales teams, where members work in pods to enhance collaboration and communication. This structure allows for flexibility and shared expertise, making it easier to manage workload and support each other.

Closing Remarks and Questions (49:30 - 55:20)
Armando addresses questions about the "founder pixie dust" concept, explaining that founders often have a unique ability to connect with customers and convey vision. He discusses compensation structures for sales roles, recommending a balanced approach between base salary and variable compensation, especially in early hires.
