**Description:** 2023 portfolio metrics benchmarking analysis, findings include institutional and consumer models.
**Tags:** revenue, growth, retention

We are excited to share the results of our 2023 portfolio metrics benchmarking analysis. This is our first time conducting this analysis since 2019, as we took a pause during the turbulent COVID years. While some of the turbulence has persisted into the period covered by this analysis (as you can see below and in the report), we hope it will still be helpful and relevant as you navigate the new normal.
We report median, 75th percentile, and 90th percentile benchmarks across a broad range of metrics and segmentations. The benchmarks represent meaningful respondent representation across institutional (N = 36) and consumer (N = 27) business models, as well as the Pre-Seed/Seed (N = 25), Series A (N = 21), and Series B+ (N = 17) stages which constitute the core of the Reach portfolio.
Findings include:
- Institutional (B2B)
  - Revenue growth decreases with maturity but remains positive and healthy: Median YoY revenue growth rates are 1.7x (i.e. 70% growth), 1.5x, and 1.3x for Pre-Seed/Seed, Series A, Series B+ companies, respectively.
  - Revenue per FTE increases dramatically as companies mature: Median revenue/FTE is $60K, $119K, and $201K for Pre-Seed/Seed, Series A, and Series B+ companies, respectively.
  - Retention is lower and trends backwards to typical benchmarks: For example, median NRR is 100%, 94, and 93% for the SMB, Mid-Market, and Enterprise segments, respectively, compared to 2019 benchmarks where there was a clear upward trend across the segments and the latter two were well above 100%. This is likely due to post-COVID turbulence in the B2B market, which includes K-12 schools and districts, universities, and corporates.
- Consumer (B2C)
  - Revenue growth does not show a monotonic leveling off by stage: Median YoY revenue growth rates are 1.7x, 1.9x, and 1.2x for Pre-Seed/Seed, Series A, Series B+ companies, respectively, reflecting the more diverse range of end markets, GTM channels, and growth dynamics represented by consumer companies in the Reach portfolio across the maturity continuum.
  - Product offering impacts gross margin but is not deterministic: Median gross margin is 57% and 78% for services and software companies, respectively, but 90th percentile services companies have a gross margin of 89%.
  - Consumer companies have higher cash needs: Consumer companies at the Series A and Series B+ have significantly higher net burn than their institutional counterparts, and unlike their institutional counterparts, even 90th percentile consumer companies still burn cash at every stage.
