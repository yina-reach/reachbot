The Shifting Landscape of TV Streaming
- The need to diversify marketing spend beyond social (often 50-80% of budget) for ROI is critical.
- The next decade will be "the decade of connected TV advertising," offering performance marketing capabilities similar to pre-iOS 14 social channels.
- Streaming and linear TV now have comparable reach for 18-49 year olds and parents, but streaming hours have surged almost 400% while linear remained static or declined.
- Younger audiences (18-34) predominantly stream; even "marquee live events" like the Grammys saw linear decline (-2%) while streaming grew by over 100%.
- Roku leads the market with over 60 million active accounts and 20.9 billion streaming hours (Q4 2021), demonstrating massive scale for reach and engagement.
Engaging Families and Kids on Roku
- As of 2021, kids are more likely to be reached by streaming than traditional TV, highlighting a crucial gap for brands not advertising in this space.
- Roku's Kids & Family content saw significant growth in 2021, with a 44% increase in household reach (40.5 million people) and nearly 90% increased engagement.
- The launch of a dedicated Kids & Family tile in summer 2021 drove 40% of watch time in Q4, emphasizing the importance of discoverability for age-appropriate content.
- Roku provides a unique co-viewing opportunity: over 59% of parents watch daily with their kids on The Roku Channel, making it a prime "family time" advertising slot.
- The "pester power" or "nag factor" of kids significantly influences parental purchases for toys and family products, even if direct measurement is limited in this segment.
- Roku is investing in exclusive content, diverse talent, and Spanish-language programming within its Kids & Family offerings to meet user demand.
Roku OneView: The Unified Ad Platform
- Roku commands a leading 45% share of all TV streaming ad impressions, surpassing platforms like Amazon, Google, and Apple TV.
- OneView is Roku's unified ad buying platform, providing a "complete view of the TV universe" to reach consumers who spend over 99 minutes daily streaming.
- The platform centralizes inventory from multiple sources: Roku Media (The Roku Channel, Roku's AVOD), Roku Publisher Media (Hulu, Peacock, other AVODs), and other CTV Media (Google, Vizio, Amazon TVs powered by Roku OS).
- Roku's operating system connection enables cross-device capabilities, allowing advertisers to target consumers across TV, desktop, mobile, audio, and native, and manage reach/frequency in one place.
- Roku's 20-year history of building consumer trust and first-party data makes its identity graph resilient against increasing privacy restrictions (e.g., IDFA, future IP address changes).
Roku Core 6: Driving Performance & Acquisition
- The "Roku Core 6" framework guides advertisers in leveraging OneView to achieve new customer acquisition and drive performance.
- Brands can upload their first-party data (privacy-compliant) or utilize Roku's own first-party data, with 40% of consumers discovering new brands on the platform.
- Roku's consumer-focused identity provides a deterministic link to the same consumer across TV, desktop, and mobile, ensuring accurate targeting and measurement.
- Cross-device capabilities allow campaigns to find and engage consumers across the entire digital ecosystem, extending reach beyond the TV screen.
- OneView's machine learning algorithms are specifically built for TV streaming, optimizing to deliver the right message to the right consumer at the right time.
- Robust analytics and incrementality testing provide clear insights into campaign performance and the acquisition of net new customers.
- Case Study: Lovevery, a subscription toy company, saw a 72% lift in site visits and 170% lift in purchases by fully implementing all Roku Core 6 principles.
Understanding Incrementality for ROI
- Attribution measures correlation (touchpoints leading to conversion), useful for real-time optimization to achieve CAC goals.
- Incrementality measures causal impact (did media *cause* the conversion), providing strategic insights for business decisions. It requires ~30 days to stabilize and is set up pre-campaign.
- Roku employs a "ghost ads" methodology (similar to Google) where a control group of eligible ad opportunities is *not* served ads but monitored for conversion behavior.
- This method quantifies the true "lift," identifying purchasers who "never would have occurred if not for your OTT media," proving incremental value over other channels.
- Roku's reporting includes households reached, converted, conversion rates for both treatment and control groups, highlighting incremental actions and lift percentages.
- The full-funnel approach often reveals strong lower-funnel KPIs and incremental actions from OTT campaigns, challenging initial perceptions of streaming as purely an awareness play.
Strategic Advice for Founders
- Build Video Muscle: Video is becoming crucial for both user acquisition and brand building; all companies, even early-stage, should invest in developing this marketing capability.
- Creative Strategy: Best practices include strong branding upfront in videos, clear calls to action, and adapting creative for cross-device strategies (e.g., video for TV, banners for mobile retargeting).
- Test & Learn Mindset: Success in OTT, similar to early mobile advertising, is a "measurement problem" that requires iterative testing and optimizing based on data.
- Investment for Significance: While not as low-cost to start as social ($40/day), sufficient investment is needed to achieve statistical significance and allow machine learning to optimize effectively.
- Future Self-Serve Options: Roku is developing a more self-serve tool with lower minimums, slated for Q4/early next year, specifically for growth marketers and VC-backed companies.
- Strategic Channel: Roku positions streaming as a strategic channel for VC-backed companies to test and learn, offering the potential to replicate the growth found on search and social by proving incremental value.
