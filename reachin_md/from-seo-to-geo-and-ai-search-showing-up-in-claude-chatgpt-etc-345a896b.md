From SEO to AI Search: How to Show Up in ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini
1. Introduction & Background
  - 30+ years in online marketing
  - Built and sold a gaming startup (10M users)
  - Roles at IGN/News Corp properties
  - B2B pivot: Salesforce → first marketing hire/CMO at Zendesk (IPO) → CMO at Slack (50 ppl → IPO; later led all customer-facing teams)
  - Defines CMO as owning the holistic customer journey; branding = sum of all touchpoints your customers experience
1. Why talk about AIO (AI Optimization) now
  - SEO traffic is declining as users move to AI assistants/search
  - Emergence of AIO/GEO: optimizing for LLM answers, not just 10 blue links
1. Core philosophy: customer-centric, helpful content > brochure-ware
  - AI favors content that explains concepts, answers questions, teaches frameworks
  - Litmus test: “Is this genuinely helpful, or just ‘10 reasons we’re awesome’?”
  - Example: Salesforce’s “social enterprise” homepage copy confused users, hurt conversions
1. Ten AIO tactics (how to show up in AI answers)
  - Clear, AI-readable positioning: Avoid jargon and vague “next-gen platform” language; write so anyone understands what you do
  - Consistency across the web (“consensus proof”): Same description on website, review sites, analyst reports, podcasts, profiles, etc. Models look for repeated patterns across sources
  - Third‑party validation > press: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Reddit, Hacker News, GitHub, etc. Use NPS promoters (9–10) to fuel reviews, referrals, customer stories
  - Authoritative, evergreen content: “What is X” pages, comparisons, implementation guides, glossaries, FAQs
    - Example: Zendesk’s “best interview questions for hiring a customer support agent”
  - Original data / distinctive POV: Benchmark reports, usage data, surveys, efficacy studies, proprietary frameworks
  - Structured Q&A / FAQ format: Explicit question–answer sections, FAQ pages, headings phrased as questions
  - Founder / expert amplification: Founders as “authority carriers” across LinkedIn, X, Reddit, podcasts, etc.
  - Video as a high‑leverage format: YouTube content being transcribed and indexed by models
    - His SaaS CMO Pro channel gets surfaced even without companion blog posts
    - Use tools like Opus Clip to atomize long videos into shorts
  - Community participation: Being referenced thoughtfully in communities (Reddit, etc.) vs just broadcasting
    - Example: Eero founder answering user questions on Reddit
  - Classic SEO hygiene still matters: Fast, clean, crawlable site; clear structure; transcripts for videos on their own pages
1. How to use AI to create content for AIO
  - Use models (Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) to:
    - Generate topic ideas and outlines rooted in your ICP’s pain points
    - Draft helpful, non-salesy educational content
  - Emphasize uniqueness and usefulness over AI-generated volume
1. Attribution in an AI-led world
  - Some companies now see 5–7% (likely more) of leads coming “via ChatGPT”
  - Hard to track because answers often mention brands without a click
  - Ideas:
    - “How did you hear about us?” survey with AI channels as options
    - Track AI-referral where links are present
    - Accept that attribution will be imperfect and multi-touch
1. Implications for hiring marketing leaders
  - Marketing tenure is short; role is broad and failure-prone if expectations are fuzzy
  - Don’t default to “first hire = CMO”; consider Dir/VP level depending on stage
  - Clarify your biggest pain point (awareness, PLG motion, sales enablement, etc.)
  - Hire for one spike (their superpower) rather than expecting a unicorn who does everything
  - AI and lower barriers to building SaaS mean more competition → marketing becomes more critical as a differentiator
1. Product-led growth (PLG) mindset and metrics
  - PLG definition: more of the journey happens inside the product than via sales/site
  - Old B2B metrics (leads, MQLs, opportunities) are insufficient alone
  - PLG‑aligned metrics:
    - Daily Active Users (DAU)
    - Net Promoter Score (NPS)
    - Product Qualified Leads (PQLs): usage + firmographic/fit signals
  - Need to infuse marketing, sales, and support into the product experience (e.g., in-product education and messaging)
1. North Star metrics & cross-functional alignment
  - At Slack: company‑wide focus on DAU, NPS, and ARR
  - Shared metrics reduce silos/politics and enable cross-team initiatives
  - Example: Slack 5M DAU stretch goal → creative, company‑wide push; when hit, everyone got a week off
  - At Zendesk: in‑product education modules required product + marketing alignment
1. Prioritization across channels
  - AIO is one channel among many; you don’t need to do all 10 tactics at once
  - Pick 2–3 channels/plays you can execute really well instead of spreading thin
  - Weigh capital efficiency: effort/cost vs impact
