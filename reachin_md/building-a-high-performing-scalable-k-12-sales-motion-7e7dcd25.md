Learn and discuss how competency-based hiring, organizational alignment, a buyer-centric sales process, and developing an ESSA-aligned evidence base all play key roles in fueling a high performing sales function. 
Introduction (00:29 - 02:52) 
John introduces himself and his extensive experience in EdTech, specifically in go-to-market sales. He aims to share insights on creating a scalable and repeatable sales process in the K-12 EdTech space.

Agenda and Key Topics (05:26 - 06:54) 
The session focuses on actionable recommendations for founders and leadership teams in early to growth-stage firms. Key areas include market challenges, keys to success, and developing a high-performing, scalable sales process.

Acknowledging K-12 Market Challenges (06:20 - 06:34) 
John emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the unique challenges in the K-12 market to effectively develop and execute a go-to-market strategy.

Developing a Sales-Centric Culture (10:35 - 11:54) 
The conversation shifts to the importance of cultivating a sales-centric culture. John highlights the need to focus on the individuals, team dynamics, and engagement models that support the sales function and overall strategy.

Hiring and Assessing Candidates (15:04 - 18:20) 
John shares best practices for hiring sales candidates, particularly emphasizing the importance of executive rapport as a core competency. He recommends engaging with customer references to assess a candidate's ability to build trust and influence decisions.

Trade Shows and Events (20:39 - 24:17) 
Discussion on the strategic participation in trade shows and events to engage with district leaders and vendors. John talks about scheduling ancillary events and leveraging data to provide best practice recommendations and build trust.

Steps Toward a Scalable Sales Process (24:30 - 37:45)
John outlines specific steps for creating a scalable and repeatable sales process. Key points include engaging with key buyer personas, crafting compelling messages, and developing elevator pitches tailored to different stakeholders.

Sales Process Stages and CRM Configuration (44:15 - 45:29)
Importance of defining stages in the sales process and configuring CRM systems to reflect buyer processes. John stresses validating sales opportunities to forecast accurately.

Initial Prospect Conversations (45:29 - 51:39) 
Tips for nailing the first conversation with prospects. John discusses the importance of understanding the prospect's customer and tailoring the script to provide value.

Evidence-Based Selling (59:48 - 1:04:17)
John concludes with the importance of using evidence to build trust and credibility in the K-12 space. He suggests training sales teams on the ESSA framework and leading with evidence of efficacy in marketing and sales efforts.
