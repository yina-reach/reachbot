Comms: Accelerating Business Rhythm
- Internal communication is fundamental but often overlooked; it becomes critical as startups grow from 5 to 50+ people.
- Effective comms ensure the right people have the right information at the right time, preventing misalignment and missed opportunities.
- Great internal comms act as "rhythm accelerators," leading to clearer information, better-informed decisions, and faster progress.
- Maximizing progress during your typical 18-24 month capital runway can be the difference between a sustainable business and a dead one.
Core Goals of Internal Comms
- Alignment: Ensure perfect understanding of intentions and direction from leadership down, with clear priorities, KPIs, and OKRs visible to all.
- Feedback: Foster "psychological safety," allowing team members to take interpersonal risks, be creative, and challenge ideas without fear of judgment.
- Connection: Build the desired company culture; the CEO's job is to be extraordinary at internal comms in service of the business and its culture.
- Resolution: Achieve clarity and closure on strategic decisions (e.g., growth vs. runway, high burn hiring vs. cutbacks), effectively break ties, and resolve both personal and tactical conflicts.
Four Critical Comms Artifacts
- Leverage four core artifacts to help your company move faster; these require regular, periodic review and updating ("gardening metaphor").
- Avoid over-reliance on email and Slack, as they fail on scalability and lack discoverable context for new teammates.
- The essential communication artifacts are: Org Chart, Meeting Calendar, Knowledge Database (Wiki), and KPI Dashboard.
Design Your Org Chart Strategically
- Your org chart dictates company movement and agility, enabling distributed decision-making across functional areas.
- As CEO, delegate decision-making; when people come to you with problems, redirect them to the designated decider while offering support.
- Rule 1: Align on Strategy & Passion, Not Ego. Structure should support business strategy, and leaders must be genuinely passionate about their domain (e.g., avoid giving an engineering team to a non-technical leader who doesn't care about their growth).
- Rule 2: Ensure Vertical Goal Alignment. Goals and metrics within a given group must be consistent, not conflicting (e.g., avoid incentivizing engineers not to fail, which can halt progress).
- Rule 3: Distribute Low Leverage Tasks. Minimize taxing senior leaders with tasks that can be delegated to more junior individuals or individual contributors.
- Rule 4: Apply the Rule of Eight. A leader should ideally have no more than 7-8 direct reports to effectively provide feedback, resolution, alignment, and connection.
Master Your Meeting Calendar
- Meetings are a significant expense but are invaluable "rhythmic accelerants" for synchronizing communication and achieving goals.
- Daily Standups: 15-minute quick check-ins, often functional-specific, for rapid issue identification and taking resolution offline. CEOs should selectively participate in high-leverage standups or occasionally skip-level to observe and learn.
- Weekly One-on-Ones: 30-60 minutes (depending on seniority) dedicated to issue resolution, individual growth, and progress to goal. As a leader, "the more you talk, the less you learn."
- Utilize the PEACE framework for 1:1s: Presence (on time, no distractions), Empathy (care for the employee), Agenda (encourage employee ownership), Check-in (relational discussion), and Exchange (employee shares, then leader shares top-down company info).
Build Smart Knowledge & KPI Systems
- Maintain a Knowledge Database (Wiki) as a searchable archive for decisions, goals, and processes to provide context and aid ramp-up for all team members, especially new hires.
- While Google Docs/Sheets are excellent for real-time editing, link them within your Wiki/Notion and add sufficient context to ensure discoverability. Notion is ideal for hierarchy and organization.
- Implement a KPI Dashboard that clearly shows "what's measured and what's not measured" across teams.
- Share cross-functional KPIs (e.g., sales can view marketing's) to foster transparency and enable proactive assistance across departments.
- For building cross-team trust and feedback, consider "trust exercises" by asking, "What is X's single most important quality that contributes to/detracts from this team's strength?" or using personality frameworks like StrengthsFinder for rapid intimacy.
