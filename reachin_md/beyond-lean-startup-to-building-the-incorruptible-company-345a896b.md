### Book Genesis and Motivation
- Wrote Incorruptible after witnessing repeated pattern/perceived inevitability of companies become unrecognizable by success - founders kicked out, lose control
- Personal experience: tasted private equity takeover in restaurant food quality decline
  - Two different PE firms had ruined two restaurants within a mile of each other
### Core Thesis: Best Practices Are Value-Destroying
- Multiple corruption vectors:
  - Financial pressures
  - Mission drift
  - Governance failures
  - Cultural degradation
  - Board interference
  - Structural legal entity problems
- A better way exists: mix of new techniques and old-fashioned methods
### Anthropic Case Study: Spiritual Holding Company Structure
- Major companies (50% of Copenhagen Stock Exchange by market cap) exist with this structure: Hershey, Patagonia, REI, LEGO, IKEA, Novo Nordisk, etc.
  - 6x more likely to reach year 50 (60% vs. 10% survival rate)
- Long Term Benefit Trust (LTBT) - perpetual purpose trust under Delaware law
  - Took Anthropic 2 years, wrote into charter for investors through Series C
- Structure protects mission and enables courage - walked away from $200M contract to maintain principles
- Trustworthiness drives commercial benefits and talent attraction
### Warning: “Always Too Early Until Too Late”
- Lawyers/advisors consistently say “too early” at every stage
  - Seed: “Wait until product-market fit”
  - Series A: “Don’t antagonize investors”
  - Growth: “Don’t be too different”
  - IPO prep: “Land the plane first”
  - Post-IPO: “You should have said something - now it’s too late”
- Whatever stage you’re at, better to act now than wait
- Success creates liability, not freedom
  - Things that make an org valuable make it worth stealing
  - Need structural protection before pressure hits
- Most vulnerable when company comes under external pressure
### Costco vs FedMart: The Golden Goose Parable
- Sol Price founded FedMart with revolutionary retail philosophy
  - Fiduciary duty to customers, capped 14% margins, even posted competitors’ lower prices in his own stores
- 1975: Investors changed locks, fired him after 20 years, and reverted to conventional retail practices for faster growth
- FedMart bankrupt by 1982
- Price founded Price Club upstairs from FedMart office
- Jim Senegal (Price Club exec) then founded Costco using same principles
  - Added “governance fortress” - structural protections FedMart lacked
  - $400B valuation today despite governance experts giving lowest possible scores
### Financial Pressures and Founder Leverage
- In the current environment “overrun with mercenaries," founders have more leverage than they think
- Mission should be core, not afterthought
  - Anthropic told investors upfront about mission-driven structure, attracted aligned investors
- People who respond to mission feel “seen and heard” - creates magnetic alignment
