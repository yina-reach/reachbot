Kickstart Meetings Effectively
- **Red Yellow Green Check-in**: Use a traffic light metaphor (Red: blocked, Yellow: distracted, Green: ready) to quickly name your current state and be present for the meeting.
- **Non-judgmental Approach**: There's no judgment; simply acknowledge where you are. For a "Red" state, ask if anything is needed to be present rather than trying to fix it.
- **Concise Context**: Share a brief sentence of context (or none at all) to settle everyone in within the first 3-4 minutes of a meeting.
Reflect on Leadership Challenges
- **Identify Significant Challenges**: Pinpoint your most pressing leadership challenge, considering both urgent and important aspects.
- **Focus on Agency**: Determine where you have the most direct control or influence to enact change within that challenge.
- **Rethink Your Approach**: Actively consider how you would like to tackle the situation differently, fostering new strategies.
- **Value Peer Input**: Even short peer discussions can provide fresh perspectives and help refine your proposed solutions.
- **Practice Active Listening**: When others share, listen intently without attempting to fix or solve their problems.
Understanding Leadership Coaching
- **Transform Mental Models**: Coaching helps leaders make their unconscious mental models conscious, creating choice and leading to better outcomes aligned with their values.
- **Outside Perspective, Inner Resilience**: A coach acts as a mirror, asking powerful questions to enhance self-observation and cultivate your resilience, rather than fostering dependence.
- **Distinct Modalities**: Coaching focuses on the present and future (action-oriented inquiry), differentiating it from therapy (past, diagnosis), advising (experience, telling), or consulting (outsourcing solutions).
- **Physics, Not Engineering**: Coaching often operates at the "physics" level of human and organizational dynamics (e.g., power, communication) and partners on "engineering" specific solutions rather than prescribing them.
Choosing Your Coaching Fit
- **IQ vs. EQ Continuum**: Coaches range from high IQ (former operators, advisors leaning on experience) to high EQ (therapy/psychology backgrounds focusing on inner work).
- **Reboot's Hybrid Approach**: Aims for a balance, combining coaches with operational/investor experience with those skilled in deep identity-level inquiry ("radical self-inquiry").
- **Assess Your Needs**: Consider whether you primarily seek practical advice, deep personal exploration, or a blend of both in a coach.
When & Why to Engage a Coach
- **Identify a Growth Edge**: The ideal time is when you sense you're bumping against your current leadership limitations, ideally before feeling overwhelmed or burnt out.
- **Scale with Your Company**: Seek coaching when "what got you here won't get you there," indicating a need to expand your leadership toolkit to match company growth.
- **Genuine Readiness**: The highest ROI comes from an "inner hunger" to change and grow, not just engaging a coach because it's a trend or expectation.
- **Evaluate ROI**: While hard to quantify precisely, consider your increased effectiveness (e.g., 10-25% improvement) against your payroll responsibilities or monthly burn rate.
- **Flexible Commitment**: Most engagements are month-to-month, allowing you to continually monitor the return on your investment.
Coaching for Team Dynamics & Solutions
- **Leverage Peer Practices**: Actively participate in peer coaching groups to develop transferable leadership skills by both giving and receiving support and insights.
- **Strategic Team Coaching**: Engaging a coach to work with leadership teams over several months or quarters can significantly improve team cohesion and effectiveness.
- **Client-Led Solutions**: Coaches prioritize empowering you to discover your own solutions, as these are more motivating, values-aligned, and effective than prescribed advice.
- **Inquiry Precedes Advice**: Coaches typically lead with inquiry ("What would be helpful here?") and will only offer direct advice after explicitly asking permission from the client.
Funding Your Coaching Journey
- **Company-Funded Development**: Most commonly, companies fund coaching as a professional development expense for their leaders.
- **Joint Investor/Company Funding**: In some cases, an investor may co-fund the coaching engagement with the company.
- **Self-Funding (Less Common)**: While possible, individual self-funding for coaching is the least common payment method.
