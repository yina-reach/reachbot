Navigating the ESSER Cliff
- **Obligation Deadline:** All ESSER funds must be *obligated* by September 30, 2024. This means a contract must be signed or a purchase order issued by this date.
- **Delivery Window:** Products and services obligated by the deadline can be delivered and paid for up to 14 months *after* September 30, 2024.
- **Remaining Funds:** Approximately $67 billion in American Rescue Plan (ARP) ESSER funds are still available to be obligated. Expect "hyper-buying" in Spring 2024.
- **Tracking Funds:** Use the Education Stabilization Fund portal (which is being updated) to track ESSER, HEER, EANS (private schools), and GEER (Governor's Emergency Education Relief Fund) money by state.
- **EANS Opportunity:** Consider targeting private schools via EANS funds. Many students have shifted from public to private, making it a viable, albeit smaller, market.
State & Local Funding Priorities
- **Review ARP State Plans:** States developed five-year plans for their last third of ARP funds. Focus on the "highlights" sections of these plans to understand state-level priorities.
- **Align with Local Plans:** District plans must align with state plans. Look for links to local plans within state documents for insights into specific district spending priorities.
- **Targeted Sales:** Use state and local plans to identify "low-hanging fruit" opportunities where your product aligns directly with stated priorities (e.g., states prioritizing Social-Emotional Learning or Mental Health).
- **Dynamic Priorities:** Be aware of shifting state priorities, such as new mandates like the "science of reading," which can dictate where funds are funneled.
Understanding Core Federal Funding
- **Post-ESSER Stability:** The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) is the primary federal funding source post-ESSER. It's not expected to be reauthorized in the near future.
- **Discretionary Funds:** Federal money (8-10% of total K-12 budgets) offers the most flexibility for product purchases, as state and local budgets are largely allocated to personnel (85%).
- **Monitor Key Titles:** Track budget changes for major ESSA programs like Title 1 (low-income students), Special Education, Title 2 (professional development), Title 4A (student support), and CTE (Career & Technical Education).
- **Budget Outlook:** The President's proposed 2024 budget shows stable or slight increases for ESSA titles, indicating Congress is unlikely to make huge changes given the remaining ESSER funds.
Addressing K-12 Challenges
- **Absenteeism Crisis:** Chronic absenteeism (17-25% in some areas) is a significant challenge but also an opportunity for solutions addressing student engagement and attendance.
- **Teacher Shortages:** Critical shortages are leading some districts to hire non-credentialed staff (e.g., bus drivers) as teachers, creating a massive need for targeted professional development.
- **Recruitment Incentives:** ESSER funds are being used for one-time signing bonuses for new teachers, not for recurring salaries.
- **Government Shutdown Fears:** Potential government shutdowns can cause districts to halt purchasing as they hoard reserves, fearing impacts on future ESSA funding (though not ESSER).
- **Parental Engagement:** Increased parental rights and influence over curriculum can impact spending decisions, requiring vendors to be sensitive to community dynamics.
High-Growth Investment Areas
- **Mental Health (Not SEL):** Market solutions as "mental health" support, as the term "SEL" is politically sensitive and restricted in some states.
- **Reading & Math Recovery:** Focus on products addressing learning loss. Align with "science of reading" mandates (e.g., Ohio, Tennessee) and prepare for emerging "science of math" discussions.
- **Tutoring Initiatives:** A potential White House special fund for reading and math tutoring creates opportunities, especially for professional development or online courses given tutor shortages.
- **Workforce Preparedness & CTE:** Develop offerings that lead to certificates (post-high school, pre-community college) to meet industry demand for specific skills.
- **Early Childhood Education:** Preschool and transitional kindergarten are massive growth areas with significant funding (e.g., $4B in California for TK), primarily needing professional development for staff.
- **School Safety:** Funding extends beyond physical security to include mental health, SEL-adjacent solutions, and other student well-being initiatives.
Vendor Sales Strategy & Funding
- **Educate Prospects:** Assume district staff (even high-level ones) may not have an encyclopedic knowledge of all federal funding streams. Be prepared to educate them on applicable funds.
- **Align Products to Funds:** Frame your product's value in terms of specific federal fund eligibility (e.g., Title 2 for PD, Title 4A for student support, CTE funds for workforce prep).
- **Understand Fund Commingling:** ESSER funds can often be commingled with other federal funds (e.g., Title 1, Title 3 for English learners) for large purchases, but percentages must align with student demographics. *Special Education funds cannot be commingled.*
- **Provide "How to Buy" Resources:** Emulate large incumbents by featuring clear landing pages or sales materials that explain how to procure your tools using different funding sources.
- **Summer Program Opportunities:** Regular Title funds can be carried over for summer programs, extending purchasing opportunities beyond the traditional school year.
- **Multi-Year Contracts:** While technically not allowed, some districts are attempting to use remaining ESSER funds for multi-year subscriptions (e.g., 5-year contracts) on the last day of the obligation period.
AI in Education: Policies & Marketing
- **Congressional Scrutiny:** Congress is actively holding hearings to define AI in education and develop policies. Be aware that the regulatory landscape is evolving.
- **Thoughtful Integration:** Avoid generic "our product has AI" marketing. Instead, partner with districts to educate them on *what* AI is, its specific benefits (e.g., for lesson planning), and how your product uses it thoughtfully.
- **Address Privacy & Ethics:** Transparently address critical issues like data privacy, data handling, and ethical use of AI, as these are major concerns for school districts.
- **Be an Educational Partner:** Position your company as a thoughtful, intellectual partner helping districts navigate and understand AI, rather than just a vendor pushing a product.
Enrollment & Choice Program Impacts
- **Shrinking Budgets:** Public school districts face dramatically shrinking budgets due to significant declines in enrollment, compounded by the ESSER cliff.
- **ESA Uncertainty:** The exact impact of Education Savings Accounts (ESAs) and vouchers on public school funding is still "to be determined" (TBD), as no one knows precisely how student and funding shifts will net out.
- **Monitor Consolidated Reports:** Watch for district consolidated reports (due end of September/October 2024), which will provide crucial data on student counts that determine federal funding levels.
- **Parental Withdrawals:** Teacher shortages and issues like "zoom in the room" learning are contributing factors to parents withdrawing children from public schools in higher numbers.
