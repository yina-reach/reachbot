GOOD TROUBLE
Strategic Advisors
Collaborating with good, smart people to build organizations the world needs
Jeff Liberty is Good Trouble’s Founding President and CEO. Over a distinguished career spanning four decades, Jeff has developed extensive expertise in supporting the launch and maturation of mission-driven organizations.
An educational entrepreneur with over 20 years of experience as a teacher, district administrator, literacy coach, and the founder of a new green-themed secondary school in Boston, Jeff joined the BetterLesson team in 2014 and played a critical role in the growth of the company over ten years where he served as BetterLesson’s Senior Vice President of Innovation and most recently as its National Vice President of Urban and Strategic Partnerships.
As BetterLesson's 12th employee, Jeff worked closely with the company’s founders to help pivot the organization from a scrappy, small-scale educational resources provider to an internationally respected professional learning company with a team of 300+ full- and part-time employees.
Jeff was responsible for developing, sustaining, and growing a diverse $20m+ portfolio of partners on three continents in public and private schools, districts, national foundations, and state departments of education.
Being an executive in an early or mid-stage company or nonprofit that has its sights set on growth and social impact can be a thrilling experience. It can also be very demanding and, at times, overwhelming. Good Trouble can supplement the talent and experience of CEOs, Executive Directors, and their senior leadership teams in all of the following areas:
