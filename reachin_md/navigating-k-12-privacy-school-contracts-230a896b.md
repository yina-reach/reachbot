# Navigating K-12 Privacy & School Contracts



00:41 - 03:43 Overview of Data Privacy Landscape
- The presentation addresses the complex data privacy landscape for ed tech companies.
Key federal laws include FERPA and COPPA
- States have enacted their own student data privacy laws due to perceived inadequacies in FERPA AND COPPA
- Growing importance of Data Privacy Agreements (DPAs) as contractual addendums to standard contracts.
- Audience you must address: school procurement teams, technical support teams, parents, and advocacy groups. Don’t underestimate the vocal parents.
03:43-07:00 Compliance Challenges
- Ed tech companies must navigate different laws based on their business models (vendor vs. direct-to-consumer).
- Hybrid models complicate compliance as they must adhere to multiple sets of laws.
- Emphasis on the necessity of appropriate data strategies and storage mechanisms.
07:00 - 12:15: Important Data Privacy Considerations
- State laws impose strict data use restrictions, allowing data use only for specific educational purposes.
- Data deletion obligations are critical; retaining data beyond necessity can lead to violations.
- Companies must pass down similar restrictions to subcontractors handling student data.
- Cannot engage in targeted advertising using student data.
- Clear definitions of permissible uses of student data are crucial for compliance.

12:15 - 15:27: Rise of Standard Contracts
- Introduction of state standard DPAs to streamline contracting processes.
- The NDPA from the Student Data Privacy Consortium aims to provide a uniform agreement for use across states.
- Benefits of having a standardized agreement include reduced friction in onboarding new schools.
19:03 - 24:20: Key Exhibits in SDPC’s National Data Privacy Agreement (NDPA)
- Exhibit E allows for streamlined onboarding across multiple schools within a state.
- Exhibit F outlines data security requirements, with emphasis on compliance with recognized frameworks like NIST.
- Exhibit G: State-specific terms
- Exhibit H: Company-specific modifications
24:30 - 30:14: Subcontractor Obligations
- Requirement to pass down data privacy obligations to subcontractors, but it can be difficult to ask big companies (Google, OpenAI, etc.) to follow
- Strategies for communicating data processing responsibilities to vendors.
30:59 - 35:36: AI Vendor Considerations
- Caution regarding the use of AI tools and the implications of their data retention policies.
- Importance of ensuring that student data is not used for training AI models without proper consent.
35:52 - 39:41: SOC2 Compliance and Vendor Security
- Explanation of SOC2 compliance as a measure of security practices and its relevance to schools.
- Discussion on the importance of ensuring that third-party vendors are also compliant with security standards.
39:50 - 47:19: Transparency and Communication
- Recent lawsuits from the EdTech Law Center targeting big players - Seesaw, Google, Powerschool 
- Some companies are starting to list subcontractors and what services they provide to be transparent, but there is a risk that this exposes one to litigation by zealous advocacy groups.
- Recommendations for how to present service provider information to mitigate misunderstandings.
