The Power of Pithy Messaging
- Messaging is at the core of what motivates prospects and customers to take action, driving growth for your company.
- Common founder challenges include struggling to describe the product, customers not "getting it," and outdated messaging that doesn't align across touchpoints.
- Great messaging increases consistency across teams, boosts conversion by clearly communicating value, and improves retention by supporting the customer experience.
- Three critical elements for powerful messaging: address a specific person's needs, speak to the transformation they seek (point A to B), and highlight how you are different and better than alternatives.
- These elements are unlocked by understanding the "Four Ps": Persona, Positioning, Personality, and Path.
Define Your Persona Edit Point
- Your persona is your target audience; identifying your ideal customer "edit point" is the highest requirement for strong messaging.
- For B2B, first identify your Ideal Customer Profile (firmographics, technographics), then multiple personas within those companies (e.g., frontline user, approver, CFO).
- Key insights for a strong persona include their functional, emotional, and social pains and desired gains, the trigger event that makes them seek a solution, and the blockers and motivators for action.
- When you have a multi-sided marketplace, focus your homepage on converting the #1 persona that drives traffic/growth, while clearly directing other personas elsewhere.
- To overcome blockers, use "eight proof points" like explaining how your product works differently, customer stories, third-party validation (e.g., press, influencers, awards), and data.
Craft Differentiated Positioning
- Positioning is about clearly articulating why your product is "different and better" than alternatives, focusing on unique benefits, features, or a specific segment you serve best.
- Develop a competitive map by plotting your product and key alternatives on a matrix using the main buying criteria your target customer considers.
- To "flip the script" and stand out, use three tricks: define a single "one word" attribute that contrasts competitors, create a "new frame" by introducing different buying criteria, or position your product as superior "for a specific audience."
- Formulate a Unique Selling Proposition (USP) as a one-sentence headline: "Your product helps a specific persona solve a certain problem and achieve a certain outcome through what unique way or benefits."
- Handshake example: shifted criteria from "job volume/network" (where LinkedIn won) to "early career focus/recruiters find you" (where Handshake excelled), making Handshake "LinkedIn is about your past, Handshake is about your future."
Define Your Brand Personality
- Your brand personality is "how" you communicate, acting as another key differentiator that builds affinity, rapport, and ensures consistent messaging.
- It comprises three elements: your brand character (3-5 unique attributes defining identity), tone of voice (communication style, specific words, rhythm), and visual brand identity (logo, fonts, colors, photography).
- To define brand attributes, conduct cross-functional workshops using exercises like "If our brand were an animal/city/fashion brand, what would it be?" or "If our product was a superhero, what villain are we defeating and what is our superpower?"
- For tone of voice, use "sliders" (e.g., funny/serious, casual/formal, irreverent/respectful) to align your team and reduce content revisions, ensuring consistency across all touchpoints.
- Ultimately, consolidate your persona insights, positioning, and personality elements into a style guide or brand book for team alignment.
Tailor Messaging to Customer Path
- Tailor your messaging to where your persona is on their customer journey, which typically involves five stages: Attract, Nurture, Convert, Onboard, and Engage.
- In the Attract stage, content should hook interest and open people's eyes to the problem and your solution.
- The Nurture phase is critical for building trust and desire; highlight transformation, differentiators, and customer stories to show value beyond alternatives.
- During the Convert stage, share specific product details, features, and pricing to build confidence and inspire action to sign up or buy.
- For Onboard, provide "get started" guides, support, and community access to accelerate time to value and ensure ease and delight.
- In the Engage stage, focus on driving active use, retaining customers, and expanding accounts.
- Conduct a "100 point exercise" to identify friction points in the customer journey where focused messaging and content can significantly increase conversion and growth.
Implement Your Messaging Framework
- Founders often regret not prioritizing messaging earlier; it's part of every conversation and crucial for aligning teams on customer needs and product value.
- Develop your messaging in this iterative order: Persona, Positioning, Personality, and then Path (customer journey content strategy).
- The ultimate output is a comprehensive messaging framework including your Unique Selling Proposition (USP), 3-ish key value propositions (top promises/benefits), proof points (data, stories, third-party validation) to back up each value prop, and a style guide for consistent content creation.
- A realistic timeframe to create the initial hypothesis, refine through research, and solidify this framework is approximately one quarter, with another quarter for full implementation across all marketing touchpoints.
