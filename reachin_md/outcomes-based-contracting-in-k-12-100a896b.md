00:25 Introduction to Outcomes-Based Contracting (OBC)
The session was initiated based on feedback from K-12 advisors regarding the challenges and opportunities associated with forecasting in education. The speakers, Brittany and Josh, aimed to clarify the definition of outcomes-based contracting and its relevance to district leaders.
02:30 - Understanding Outcomes-Based Contracting
The discussion focused on defining outcomes-based contracting and its implications for K-12 education. The importance of having clearly defined outcomes and mutual accountability among districts, providers, and schools was emphasized, as it drives the successful implementation of educational products and services.
07:31 - Pricing Models in EdTech
The conversation transitioned to the pricing models applicable to educational technology. The speakers highlighted the need for a pricing structure that aligns with the outcomes being targeted, moving away from traditional a la carte pricing to a more integrated approach.
10:42 - Ingredients for Successful OBC
The importance of having the right components for successful outcomes-based contracting was discussed. This includes clearly defined outcomes and a collaborative approach to ensure that all parties are aligned in their goals and responsibilities, which is crucial for achieving desired student outcomes.
15:59 - Data Sharing and Interoperability
A significant challenge mentioned was the complexity of data sharing and interoperability among different systems. The speakers noted that having clear contractual agreements upfront can facilitate better data sharing practices, which are essential for tracking progress and outcomes.
21:28 - Trends and Future of OBC
The speakers reviewed historical trends in outcomes-based contracting, starting with high-impact tutoring and outlining a three-year plan to deepen the work within their service model. They also discussed a five-year plan aimed at increasing the impact on schools.
26:29 - Feasibility Studies and Working Groups
The process of initiating outcomes-based contracting involves conducting feasibility studies and forming working groups that include various stakeholders. This collaborative approach ensures that the recommendations made are practical and grounded in real-world needs.
35:05 - Payment Structures in OBC
Josh introduced the topic of how to effectively pay for educational products under outcomes-based contracting. He discussed the differences between traditional partnerships and OBC partnerships, emphasizing the need for high-quality implementation and ongoing collaboration.
49:18 - Partnership Terms and Responsibilities
The final part of the conversation focused on the specific terms of partnerships under outcomes-based contracting. It was highlighted that both providers and districts have clear responsibilities, and failure to meet these obligations could result in financial consequences.
