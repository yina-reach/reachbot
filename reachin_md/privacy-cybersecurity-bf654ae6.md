## Education and consumer privacy regulations and best practices
🌐 [Student Privacy Compass](https://studentprivacycompass.org/)
  - Site contains a resource search center, a blog, and updated pages with specific information and guidance for parents, students educators, LEAs, SEAs, higher ed, edtech and policymakers.
🌐 [Protecting Student Privacy](https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/) | U.S. Department of Education
  - This website aims to assist stakeholders in protecting the privacy of students by providing official guidance on the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the Protection of Pupil Rights Amendment (PPRA), technical best practices and answers to Frequently Asked Questions.
📃 [Introduction to the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)](https://drive.google.com/open?id=13V3bIBA4CUf4-I5fVc7AGpA3y4USOW1g) | Orrick (2019)
  - Presentation at the SIAA and Future of Privacy Forum Student Privacy Bootcamp
📃 [California Consumer Privacy Act - What You Should Know](https://drive.google.com/file/d/14mwI9apILA-VTLUvvJL6gaRq8eBigFOy/view?usp=sharing) | Orrick (2019)
  - [Steps to prepare](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1JU19D26ijtgqk5viRyZ1O5YNmd1kcQtI/view?usp=sharing)
📃 [General Data Protection Regulation Checklist](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1oXgYBCEz6GpkyTeYF-PQDM44n0smxGnmKQHaJ6Hj2-Y/edit?usp=sharing) (GDPR) | [Adam Heller](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adheller/), June 2018
  - A list of requirements specified by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy
## Vendors and services
[Internet Keep Safe (iKeepSafe) Coalition](https://ikeepsafe.org/): Offers service that certifies digital products as compliant with state and federal requirements for handling protected personal information. They also help organizations achieve and maintain compliance through product assessments, monthly monitoring, annual training, and assistance with remediation.
[Common Sense Privacy Program](https://privacy.commonsense.org/): The Common Sense Privacy Program evaluates privacy policies so that parents and teachers can make smart choices about the learning tools they use with their children and students, and so that schools and districts can participate in improving the technology used in K–12 classrooms.
