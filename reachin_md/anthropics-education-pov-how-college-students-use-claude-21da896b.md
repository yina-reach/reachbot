# Anthropic’s Education POV & How College Students Use Claude

- https://share.fireflies.ai/embed/meetings/01JTGW5TG0AKQRRN10ASCJTNKQ
Introduction to Anthropic's Education Strategy (00:02 - 09:37)
- Tony introduced Drew Bent, who leads education research at Anthropic
- Drew has background as a teacher and EdTech founder before joining Anthropic
- Anthropic has been making explicit efforts in education, including supporting AI tool development
- Recently published research on how college students use Claude
- Drew explained Anthropic's approach as building general purpose technologies, not going deep into verticals
- Anthropic is focused on responsible AI deployment through their public benefit corporation status
- Their anonymous privacy-preserving tool 'Clio' analyzes Claude usage patterns
- Research found education is one of the top use cases for Claude (after coding)
Research Findings on Student AI Usage (09:38 - 19:25)
- Anthropic focused research on users with .edu emails to understand education usage
- Created a taxonomy framework to categorize AI interactions in education
- Framework has two dimensions: direct vs. collaborative and problem-solving vs. output creation
- Current chatbot interfaces primarily serve the 'direct problem-solving' quadrant
- Drew highlighted that alternative interfaces beyond chatbots are needed for education
- Research analyzed AI behavior using Bloom's taxonomy (remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate, create)
- Found an 'inverted pyramid' where Claude spends most time creating or analyzing for students
- Raised concerns about cognitive offloading where humans offload higher-order thinking to AI
- Evaluation skills are critical for humans but not being developed in current AI interactions
Rethinking Assessment and Academic Integrity (19:26 - 29:18)
- Discussion about redefining assessments and the concept of cheating in an AI world
- Drew emphasized the need to reconsider what skills we're teaching in an AI world
- Coding example: CS education traditionally focused on writing code, but now reviewing AI-generated code is more important
- Drew suggested two categories of assessments: AI-proof (proctored) and AI-enabled
- Anthropic isn't trying to 'conquer everything' in education but instead partner with edtech startups
- Drew urged moving beyond chatbot interfaces to more proactive AI tools for both students and teachers
- Integration with existing education tools is crucial for adoption
Education Tools and Student Adoption (29:18 - 39:29)
- Discussion about higher education institutions negotiating large-scale LLM adoption
- Institutions want multi-faceted AI strategies rather than exclusive partnerships
- Questions arose about 'bring your own API key' business models for startups
- Anthropic is developing 'Learning Mode' to make Claude better at Socratic dialogue
- Learning Mode aims to make Claude a guide rather than just answering questions
- Only about 5% of users currently prompt Claude effectively for learning purposes
- Drew emphasized the need for tools that help with pedagogically effective AI usage
- Discussion about making AI tools accessible for students with learning differences
- AI could potentially meet individual needs better than human teachers in diverse classrooms
Anthropic's Approach to Education Partnerships (39:31 - 49:48)
- AI fluency course being developed by Anthropic, to be released within a month
- Anthropic is working with K-12 edtech companies but only at the API level (Claude.com is 18+ only)
- Anthropic focuses selectively on features, emphasizing safeguards and coding models
- Discussion about how AI can help with reading level accuracy in educational content
- Drew emphasized the value of human-to-human connection in education
- Best AI applications in education deepen human connections or free up time for human interaction
- Example: Using AI to analyze tutoring sessions and improve teaching techniques
Future of Education with AI (49:48 - 58:18)
- Discussion about illusion of competence and how Learning Mode addresses this
- Feature suggestion from attendee: Connecting different Claude instances used by the same student
- Drew believes AI could fundamentally change what and why we teach
- Drew suggested AI tools will likely become more affordable and accessible over time
- Anthropic is developing partnerships with universities to increase accessibility
- Drew predicted that if AI can handle most cognitive tasks in the near future, education might return to its roots of building community and citizenship
