Equity & Student Outcomes
- Districts are doubling down on DEIAB (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Access, and Belonging) initiatives, including courageous conversations training for all staff, equity audits, and revamping HR for diversity hires.
- Strategic plans prioritize equity, breaking down opportunity barriers, and ensuring a rigorous education for all students daily, moving beyond "symbolic feel-good activities."
- Key wins include curriculum alignment (e.g., K-3 common page, K-5 math/ELA, Science of Reading), master schedule overhauls to remove access barriers (e.g., elementary electives during school day).
- Amplifying student voice is a focus, leading to initiatives like Black Student Unions (110+ students, expanding across grades) and superintendent advisory councils.
- Districts are creating authentic experiences, such as an annual Historically Black Colleges Tour for 300 students from across the county.
Addressing Student Well-being
- Post-pandemic, districts are seeing unprecedented student behaviors and emotional struggles, alongside a fatigued workforce with lower daily attendance among teachers.
- Significant investments are being made in mental health support, including adding social workers to every building to complement psychologists.
- Summer learning programs are being reimagined, moving away from purely academic remediation to a "camp experience" that addresses academic skills while providing enrichment opportunities to close access gaps.
- While some districts measure zero ROI on academic summer programs, the pivot to engagement and community service still provides value.
The AI Integration Imperative
- Most districts are in the "infancy" stage of AI adoption; teachers are often "terrified" (85%) or "invigorated" (15%), with a significant fear of content becoming irrelevant or being used for cheating.
- Administrative use of AI is robust for efficiency (e.g., drafting letters, generating speech ideas), with some calling it "malpractice" not to leverage accessible tools.
- Key uses include differentiating instruction by quickly generating varied problems based on student interests and streamlining administrative tasks like writing special education goals.
- Superintendents emphasize the need to understand the rapid pace of change ("10-20 months, not 10-20 years") and shift instructional practices to prepare students for "what might be," rather than "what was."
Strategic Funding & Partnerships
- Districts leveraged SR (Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief) funding for initial security investments (protecting systems, student data) but face future challenges with maintenance and subscription costs.
- There's a focus on making "thoughtful choices" about which products truly add value, as the money "starts to go away."
- Superintendents express a strong dislike for multi-year contracts if products don't perform, advocating for good-faith partnerships where vendors modify products or "bow out" if they aren't working.
- Despite funding uncertainties, technology is seen as essential for future learning, and districts "must invest in tech tools" and provide teacher training to open future doors for students.
Navigating District Change & Culture
- Community pushback and "culture wars" can be significant challenges, leading to "messy and nasty" board elections that erode the district's reputation as a symbol of hope and pride.
- Internally, a challenge is battling "loud and obnoxious" narratives from pushback on equity work and addressing a lack of rigor/responsibility among some teachers.
- For high-performing districts, a challenge is overcoming "believing our own press clippings" and broadening the mindset to compete nationally, not just locally.
- Leadership faces the task of selling the "why" of new initiatives (like AI) to staff, rather than just the "how," and maintaining urgency during leadership transitions.
