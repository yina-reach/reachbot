What I Cover
I cover how colleges are managing — and trying to increase — enrollment and tuition revenue, which remain a critical part of their bottom lines. I’m interested in identifying strategies that are emerging (both sector-wide and at individual institutions); analyzing the structural challenges and broader headwinds working against those initiatives; and helping demystify this work for campus communities.
My Background
I joined The Chronicle in 2021, where I spent more than four years covering how college administrators are navigating an increasingly tech-infused world — especially when it comes to artificial intelligence and vendor partnerships. Prior to that, I wrote for The 74, a nonprofit news outlet, where I spearheaded K-12 coverage of the nation’s two largest school districts, NYC Public Schools and L.A. Unified, before documenting D.C. Public Schools’ pivot to virtual learning during the Covid-19 pandemic. My byline has also appeared in Newsday, Newsweek, The Hartford Courant, and on CNN.com.
I earned my bachelor’s degree in multi-platform journalism from the University of Maryland, where I got my start as a reporter and editor at The Diamondback. I live in northern Virginia and am slowly but surely adjusting to the wonderful chaos that is working-parent life.
