AWS Credit Eligibility
- Startups must be pre-Series B stage, have a functional website, and be less than 10 years old
- Startups will only be eligible to redeem credits for 12 months after their last fundraising date, which needs to be listed in their credit application to be approved.
- Startups will be eligible if they haven’t received an equal or greater amount of credits through Activate before.
Startup-Facing Instructions for Redeeming AWS Activate Credits
1. Go to the AWS Startups website: [https://aws.amazon.com/startups/credits](https://aws.amazon.com/startups/credits), Hit Apply Now. Bookmark the Startups portal (startups.aws) so you have access to programs and perks.
1. You will be asked to create a “BuilderID” – this is your personal login to the Startups portal, and is different from your AWS Account info. If you have previously applied for credits, it’s likely you already have a BuilderID login.
1. Log into your AWS account. If you have more than one account, make sure you’re signed in to the one where you’d like to receive credits.
1. Once you’ve created your BuilderID and logged in to the Startups portal, you then go to the next step to complete the credit application flow. Please apply for the Portfolio Tier credits, which is for VC-backed startups, and complete this part of the application with your business email (email account that matches your domain – it will be rejected if you use a gmail/.edu/non-business email). Please include “Seed” as the round stage, and ensure your listed raise date is in the past 12 months.
1. Use the Reach Capital $100K OrgID code (case sensitive): P3Y5J
1. Email your VC’s contact at AWS, Sergio Zafra, [zafras@amazon.com](mailto:zafras@amazon.com), to let him know you’ve applied – not required but this will expedite approvals. If they haven’t intro’ed you to us directly, cc’iing your investor can also speed things up, because it helps us confirm you’re in their portfolio.
1. Once you complete the application and are approved by the system, it will take around 7 days for you to see credits appear in your AWS console. Your credits will apply to your AWS bill for that month onward. Be sure to note the expiration date for your credits, which can be found in the AWS Console under Billing/Credits. I encourage you to monitor your credits the same way you monitor the rest of your budget.
1. Please be sure to connect with your AWS Account Team – your VC’s contact can intro you. They can help with architecture reviews, product selection, technical strategy questions, and also help connect you with GTM programs and other opportunities within Amazon. You have a number of other unlisted perks because you’re part of a top VC portfolio.
