01:19 - Yuri’s Background and Growth Marketing Journey
Yuri discusses his unexpected transition into growth marketing after the 2008 financial crisis. He explains how he joined Grammarly in its early days and contributed significantly to its growth, focusing on user acquisition and marketing strategies. His experience spans over 16 years, with a strong emphasis on performance marketing.
05:02 - Common Reasons for Stalled Growth
Yuri outlines the typical scenarios in which companies seek his advice, including those that have just raised Series A funding and need to scale. He identifies key archetypes of companies, such as those experiencing diminishing returns on existing growth levers or those aiming to achieve best-in-class growth strategies.
08:25 - Assessing Growth Strategies
He explains his approach to determining effective growth strategies, emphasizing the importance of understanding a company's product-market fit and existing growth levers. He conducts a "vitals check" to identify potential avenues for growth, such as product virality or performance marketing.
12:13 - Transitioning from Series A to Series B
Yuri discusses the challenges faced by companies moving from Series A to Series B, particularly when they rely heavily on one growth channel. He emphasizes the need to validate existing channels and explore new ones to ensure sustainable growth.
16:01 - Balancing Retargeting and Brand Building
A participant raises a question about the tension between retargeting and brand-building efforts. Yuri explains the importance of defining brand-building KPIs and suggests that brand advertising should focus on metrics like brand sentiment rather than direct user acquisition.
24:05 - Setting KPIs for Growth
Audience asks about how to set realistic KPIs for growth. Yuri advises using a triangulation approach, combining insights from investors and market benchmarks to establish achievable targets while also considering the company's current stage and resources.
29:01 - Finding Initial Growth Channels
Yuri elaborates on how to identify the most promising growth channels for early-stage companies. He emphasizes the need to evaluate which growth levers align best with the product's nature and the company's goals.
32:46 - Shifting Focus from B2C to B2B
Tony asks about Grammarly's transition from targeting students to knowledge workers. Yuri explains that this shift was driven by the challenges of monetizing academic products and the realization that a significant portion of Grammarly's usage came from non-academic content.
39:19 - Importance of First Mover Advantage
A participant inquires about the significance of first-mover advantage in new distribution channels. Yuri shares his experience at Grammarly, noting that while first-mover advantage can be beneficial, being methodical and strategic in channel selection is equally important.
52:12 - New Marketing Channels and Trends
Audience asks about emerging marketing channels. Yuri identifies influencers and SEO for LLMs (Large Language Models) as promising areas, suggesting that companies should adopt an LLM-first strategy in their SEO efforts to stay competitive.
