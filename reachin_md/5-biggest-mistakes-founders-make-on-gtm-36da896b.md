Background & Jjellyfish Overview
- Managing Partner at Jjellyfish
- 10 years experience, worked with 300+ companies
- Focus on precede/seed/Series A stage companies building repeatable outbound motions
- Three-stage framework: Feasibility → Repeatability → Scalability
  - Repeatability is the biggest challenge - building zero-to-one motion beyond warm leads
GTM Mistake #1: Hiring for Scale Before Repeatability
- Biggest mistake: hiring VP Sales/CRO before achieving product-market fit
  - Senior hires good at scalability, not zero-to-one building, often promise Rolodex connections that don’t convert
- Junior AE/SDR hires also risky: Usually executors, not strategic thinkers
- Founder must lead sales until ~$1M ARR or 8-10 similar customers
  - Only way to learn the 5-6 key pivots needed
  - Exception: hiring startup-experienced “founding AE” with founder staying involved
GTM Mistake #2: Outsourcing Sales Process
- Founders can’t completely remove themselves from sales conversations
- The Founder is the product at the early stage!
  - Customers buy vision and roadmap, not just current features
  - Founder charisma and passion critical for early deals
- Time to insight is everything - founder must be present to learn and pivot quickly
GTM Mistake #3: Over-investing in Product vs Market Development
- Technical founders sometimes default to building more features The risk: building incredible tool that solves problems nobody cares about
- First conversations should focus entirely on discovery, not product demos
- Discovery framework for every prospect conversation:
  1. Understand current situation/status quo
  1. Identify if they perceive it as a problem
  1. Quantify cost of not solving the problem
  1. Learn what they’ve tried unsuccessfully before
GTM Mistake #4: Selling Features Instead of Problems/Value
- Common pattern: 2-3 minutes conversation, then straight to demo
- People don’t buy solutions - they buy belief that you understand their problems
- Best sales reps talk 30% of time vs worst at 60%
- Proper sales narrative:
  - Deep problem discovery first
  - Reverse engineer solution to problems it solves
  - Demo only relevant features that solve their specific issues
GTM Mistake #5: Focusing on Metrics Instead of Insight
- Revenue is result, not strategy
- North Star should be continuous market learning and delivery
- Avoid vanity metrics that become proxies for progress
- Stay flexible - market tells you what to build, not other way around
Tools & Tactics Discussion
- AI impact on outreach: old personalization tactics now obvious, need better targeting
- Current effective channels: LinkedIn connection campaigns, phone calls making comeback
- Tool stack: Clay, N8N, Lemlist, Email Bison, Aircall for comprehensive outreach
- Disqualification strategy: be honest immediately when not good fit - builds long-term relationships
