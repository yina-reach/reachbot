# Scaling Innovation - Pricing, Churn & Retention

- https://share.fireflies.ai/embed/meetings/K9lH9y1BOmtTZBni
Introduction (00:01 - 01:36)
- The meeting begins with a welcome and introduction of Eddie Hartman, a partner at Simon-Kucher, known for expertise in pricing and growth strategies.
- Eddie has a background as an operator and founder, notably with LegalZoom, and is returning for a follow-up session focused on scaling growth and innovation.
Key Themes of the Presentation (01:36 - 10:12)
- Value and Pricing: Price is a measure of value, not a standalone concept. Understanding customers' willingness to pay is crucial for prioritizing product development.
- Perception of Value: Different customers perceive value differently based on context, necessitating segmentation in marketing strategies.
- Charging Models: The method of charging can significantly impact customer behavior and revenue outcomes.
Cautionary Tales of Business Failures (10:22 - 15:40)
- Eddie shares stories of three companies (Quibi, Juicero, and Ship) that failed due to a lack of balance between acquisition, monetization, and retention strategies.
- Emphasizes the importance of understanding trade-offs in business decisions to avoid pitfalls.
Understanding Churn (15:41 - 25:07)
- Types of Churn: Discusses various forms of churn, including use churn, product churn, and dollar churn, highlighting the need for businesses to monitor these closely.
- Revenue Loss: Identifying silent churn and understanding its impact on revenue is critical for sustaining growth.
Strategies for Retention (25:08 - 39:09)
- Focus Areas: Suggests focusing on the middle of the customer journey to address churn proactively rather than waiting until customers express intent to leave.
- Customer Engagement: Encourages businesses to find new pockets of need within their existing customer base and to communicate value effectively.
- Discount Strategies: Advises careful consideration of discounting practices to avoid unnecessary revenue loss.
Acquisition of the Right Customers (39:10 - 43:07)
- Stresses the importance of acquiring customers who resemble those with high lifetime value (LTV) to improve retention and profitability.
- Suggests leveraging data and insights to refine targeting strategies during the acquisition phase.
Conclusion and Q&A (43:08 - 56:20)
- Eddie opens the floor for questions, addressing concerns about pricing changes, customer acquisition strategies, and the importance of value communication.
