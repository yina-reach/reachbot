Han Li is a politics reporter for The San Francisco Standard covering local government and elections. He is bilingual in Chinese and focuses on immigration, race and equity, and U.S.–China relations.
What I Cover
I strive to bring nuance and balance to political reporting in an increasingly polarized world. I cover legislation, public policy, and the government decisions that shape civic life. I also report on campaigns and elections, examining how and why American voters shift their views across election cycles.
My Background
Fluent in Mandarin and Cantonese, I grew up in Guangzhou and studied in Taipei, Beijing, and Los Angeles before moving to San Francisco to pursue a career as a bilingual journalist. Before joining The Standard, I was a reporter at World Journal, a leading Chinese-language newspaper in the United States.
My immigrant background and language skills allow me to engage with monolingual Chinese and broader Asian American communities, uncovering stories often overlooked by mainstream media. I also report on geopolitical affairs and U.S.–China relations, one of the defining storylines of our time, and how they influence local politics.
My work has received awards from the Society of Professional Journalists’ Northern California chapter and the California News Publishers Association. I graduated from Sun Yat-sen University with a degree in journalism and earned a master’s degree from the University of Southern California.
