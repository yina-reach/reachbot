Executive Search Process Overview (02:34 - 06:13)
Helen outlines the typical timeline for an executive search, emphasizing the need for a calibration phase before officially starting the search. The importance of defining the role accurately and understanding the market is highlighted to avoid common pitfalls.

Key Stages of the Hiring Process (06:13 - 12:55)
Helen discusses the stages of the hiring process, including identifying needs, calibration, initial outreach, engagement, assessment, and selection. She stresses the significance of having a clear and intentional interview process to ensure the best candidates are identified and engaged.

Common Mistakes in Executive Hiring (12:55 - 24:46)
Helen identifies several common mistakes made during the executive hiring process, such as failing to recognize the key hire early, defining roles for the future instead of the present, and over-indexing on experience rather than motivations. She emphasizes the need for transparency and communication throughout the process to avoid delays and indecision.

Hiring from Larger Companies? (24:46 - 30:07)
The conversation touches on the challenges and considerations when hiring executives from larger companies, noting the differences in culture and expectations between large corporations and startups. Helen advises caution and thorough validation of candidates' motivations and past experiences when considering hires from big companies.

Domain Expertise Trade-offs and Mission Alignment (30:07 - 34:34)
Helen discusses the trend of hiring candidates from outside the edtech space and the importance of aligning candidates' motivations with the company's mission. She highlights the significance of finding candidates who are genuinely passionate about the mission-driven work in edtech.

Q&A (34:34 - 57:16)
The session includes a Q&A segment where participants ask about various topics, including tenure expectations for executives, sourcing candidates, and the impact of market changes on hiring trends. Helen provides insights on the importance of understanding candidate motivations and the role of informal references in the hiring process.
