**Speaker:** [Daphne Li](https://www.linkedin.com/in/daphnekli/)

00:00 - 01:07  Introduction and Focus on Privacy
Daphne expresses excitement about discussing trust and privacy in the edtech sector. They emphasize the importance of privacy as a central element in building trust with users and outline their goal to provide practical advice on when to consider privacy in business operations.
01:07 - 06:36: Overview of Privacy Regulations
The discussion shifts to the complex landscape of privacy regulations, which the speaker refers to as an "Alphabet soup." It highlights the need for companies to understand various laws, especially those related to sensitive data and student records, and mention that they will provide resources for further reference.
06:36 - 12:32: Privacy and Sales Impact
How privacy considerations can influence sales, noting that robust privacy practices can differentiate a product in the market. Examples of companies that have successfully integrated privacy into their business models, suggesting that this can inspire customer confidence and potentially accelerate sales.
12:32 - 20:11: Practical Advice on Privacy Policies
Practical steps for companies to take regarding their privacy policies, including ensuring that they are up-to-date and clearly communicate what data is collected and how it is used. Daphne stresses the importance of transparency and the need for parental consent when dealing with children's data under regulations like COPPA.
20:11 - 35:07: AI and Privacy Considerations
The conversation includes a segment on artificial intelligence, discussing how companies should handle personal and sensitive data in AI applications. Daphne emphasizes the need for transparency in data collection and the importance of conducting data protection impact assessments for systems that pose a heightened risk of harm.
35:07 - 39:28: Opportunities for Building Trust
Daphne wraps up the discussion by highlighting the opportunities that arise from investing in privacy practices. She encourages companies to view privacy not just as a regulatory burden but as a means to build trust with customers, which can ultimately lead to business growth.
39:28 - 57:51: Q&A and Closing Remarks
The session concludes with a Q&A segment where participants ask for clarification on various topics, including user-friendly privacy policies and how schools work with vendors on parental consent. The speaker reiterates their willingness to provide tailored feedback and resources to help companies navigate privacy challenges effectively.
