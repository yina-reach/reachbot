Comp Plan Psychology & Motivation
- Compensation plans, while analytical on paper, become a right-brained, emotional driver for sales reps.
- Reps prioritize hitting quota and recognition as much as, if not more than, the absolute dollar amount earned. (e.g., $175k OTE at 115% quota hit yields same pay as $225k OTE at 89%, but with higher rep satisfaction).
- Salespeople consistently discuss their compensation; assume transparency is inevitable and be prepared to communicate openly.
- Founders are responsible for setting realistic and achievable quotas, avoiding excessively high "stretch goals" that can demotivate teams.
Phased Comp Plan Evolution
- **Simplification (0-10 reps):** Keep plans extremely simple (e.g., flat 10% new business, 5% renewals, same quota for all). This reduces administration and aids understanding in early stages. Provide reps with commission calculators.
- **Differentiation (10-30 reps):** Begin varying OTEs and quotas based on role and experience. Introduce accelerators and kickers to incentivize higher performance as you bring in more experienced talent.
- **Customization (30+ reps, specialized roles):** Customize plans by role, territory, and market segment (e.g., SMB vs. Enterprise). Consider accelerators or decelerators for exceptionally large deals to manage payouts and business risk.
- **Optimization (Mature Stage):** Establish OTE bands and ground quotas in robust historical data. Fine-tune for talent retention and overall sales efficiency.
Aligning Comp with Business Goals
- Clearly define company goals (e.g., revenue growth, cash flow, customer retention) and build compensation levers that align reps with these objectives.
- Consider incentivizing reps on renewal rates or good-fit customer acquisition, not just initial bookings, to improve long-term retention.
- Aim for a 5x revenue generated per rep to salary/quota ratio; 3-4x is common for high-growth companies.
- Decide your target percentage of reps hitting quota (e.g., 20% vs. 60%) and understand the psychological implications for morale and performance.
- Carefully consider cash flow: Pay on booking (faster for reps, but higher upfront cost) versus cash collection (lower risk, but slower for reps).
Specific Comp Structure Considerations
- **COGS/Vendor Fees:** Calculate revenue generated per rep based on *top-line* revenue, not net of COGS or vendor payouts. These are business expenses, separate from sales compensation.
- **Sales Engineers/Support:** Factor their salaries into the overall Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). Measure their value through metrics like influenced deal size, close rates, or sales cycle acceleration.
- **Multi-Rep Deals:** Implement clear rules for commission splits or referral fees to handle collaborative sales efforts.
- **Clawbacks & Errors:** Minimize the use of clawbacks, especially for smaller amounts. For minor calculation errors or uncollected revenue, sometimes writing it off can significantly boost rep satisfaction and motivation.
- **Communication:** When changing compensation plans, transparently communicate the *why* and *how* to secure rep buy-in and prevent misaligned expectations.
The Role of Sales Enablement
- Sales enablement is crucial for managing the change aspects of comp plan updates.
- Ensures clear messaging and effective rollout, helping reps understand new plans, their impact, and any associated process changes.
- Develops new talk tracks, supporting documents, and sales processes aligned with new incentives (e.g., shifting from monthly to annualized contracts).
- Becomes increasingly vital in later stages (3 & 4) as comp plans become more complex and require detailed support.
