K-12 Edtech Usage Scale
- School districts (1000+ students) accessed an average of 1400 different edtech products in 2022, a significant post-COVID expansion.
- Individual educators engaged with nearly 150 different tools, and students with 143, per school year.
- Superintendents and CTOs often underestimate actual usage, typically guessing 100-300 products, highlighting a visibility gap.
- Edtech has become foundational, making education "tech-enabled" rather than an optional add-on.
Key Usage Trends & Equity Gaps
- **Growing Usage:** Tools promoting teacher-student interaction (e.g., formative assessment like Blooket, Kahoot, Nearpod), tech-enabled learning activities, and tutoring solutions are seeing increased engagement.
- **Decreasing Usage:** Reliance on "first-base" research resources like digital newspapers and encyclopedias is declining.
- The "digital learning equity gap," prevalent pre-COVID, was exacerbated by the pandemic (a "K-shaped experience") and persists, with affluent districts showing significantly higher student engagement.
- Founders should monitor the National EdTech Equity Dashboard for ongoing insights into these disparities.
ESSER Cliff & Evidence Mandate
- The upcoming ESSER funding cliff (COVID stimulus funds) is driving districts towards multi-year contracts and a greater focus on *evidence* of effectiveness.
- The Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA), passed in 2015, is increasingly dictating purchasing decisions, requiring interventions to meet one of four evidence levels.
- Federal program dollars (including ESSER/ARP) mandate that education interventions be evidence-based, creating a new standard for market entry and growth.
Navigating ESSA Evidence Levels
- **Level 4 (Demonstrates Rationale):** Start with a well-specified logic model informed by research; no product or data is technically needed to meet this initial level (e.g., using a Lean Canvas).
- **Level 3 (Promising Evidence):** Requires correlational studies, focusing on specific targets or initiatives.
- **Level 2 (Moderate Evidence):** Demands quasi-experimental studies, which include everything except randomization.
- **Level 1 (Strong Evidence):** Involves randomized control trials (RCTs); while traditionally expensive and long (IES average: 6 years, $3.5M), new services can conduct them in under a year.
Evidence-as-a-Service (EaaS) for Founders
- Learn Platform's EaaS offers affordable, rapid third-party research for edtech providers, specifically designed for ESSA compliance.
- Costs start as low as $3,000-$4,000 for initial evaluations, with annual research subscriptions in the four-to-five-figure range for ongoing studies.
- This service democratizes access to rigorous evidence, making it feasible for small organizations that previously couldn't afford it.
- ESSA "badges" (evidence designations) are distributed across platforms like Learn Platform and Canvas, providing clear visibility to educators and policymakers.
Market Adoption & Future Outlook
- While only a small percentage of 11,000 products currently have ESSA badges, adoption is growing rapidly, particularly among first-comers in competitive sectors like tutoring and core content.
- Major districts (e.g., Chicago Public Schools, LAUSD) are now including evidence requirements in RFPs and for existing vendors. States are also setting minimum ESSA levels for approved product lists.
- Founders should anticipate US Department of Education guidance in the next 2-3 months on how districts can build internal capacity for evidence-based decision-making.
- The market is shifting from skepticism to a belief that evidence-based edtech decisions are achievable and essential.
