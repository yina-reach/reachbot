# Startup Fundraising: Path to Series A & B

Fundraising Environment Overview (00:00 - 11:30)
- Fundraising environment has undergone a significant market correction
- Series A and B investments have decreased by 41% and 48% respectively
- Education sector experiencing even more severe decline
- Anemic exit environment due to IPO drought and aggressive antitrust actions
- VC consolidation with capital concentrating in big funds
- Reach Capital has ample capital available for investments
Reach Portfolio Analysis (11:31 - 21:51)
- 28 rounds done by Reach in Series A through C in 2023 and 2024
- Series A rounds averaged $11 million, Series B $20-43 million, Series C $25 million
- Reach investments align well with broader market valuations
- AI native companies commanding higher valuations
- B2B K-12 investments raised at 7-11x multiple on current ARR
- Success at Series B and C heavily dependent on perceived market size
Key Metrics and Benchmarks (21:52 - 33:03)
- Operational efficiency coupled with solid growth driving best-in-class valuations
- CAC payback and burn multiple highlighted as important metrics
- Net dollar retention and churn crucial for SaaS and EdTech companies
- LTV to CAC ratio and magic number emphasized for efficiency measurement
- B2B metrics may differ from consumer-focused businesses
Funding Strategy and Positioning (33:07 - 42:11)
- Balance between growth and profitability depends on sector and TAM
- AI companies growing faster but retention data still uncertain
- Importance of considering VC fund fit and portfolio when fundraising
- Shift from "EdTech" to broader "Learning Tech" positioning can be beneficial
Future Outlook and Q&A (42:11 - 50:51)
- K-12 curriculum still an attractive investment area for Reach Capital
- Potential increase in IPOs due to strategic acquisition limitations
- Profitability expectations vary based on company stage and growth potential
- Reach Capital team available for specific guidance and support in fundraising
