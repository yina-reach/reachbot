# Media & Press GTM

Summary
- The primary focus was on how to effectively tell stories in an oversaturated market. Betsy emphasized that businesses should aim to build trust, empathy, and their brand through storytelling but warned against struggling to cut through "noise" due to millions of blogs being published every day. She encouraged participants to think like journalists when crafting their narratives—considering what readers want rather than just pushing out information.
- George added that many PR strategies are outdated today due to changes in media consumption habits. He argued that capturing people's attention requires catching trending waves (like AI), presenting authentic learnings or insights, understanding your audience's needs/wants/interests intimately, showing instead of telling your story authentically—and even taking control by telling it yourself via platforms such as LinkedIn where you can tailor content accordingly.
- Lastly, they highlighted the value of considering who exactly you're trying to reach with different stories since investors have different interests compared to customers or peers within the industry.
- In part 2 of this transcript, the panelists discuss why journalists often write about fundraising in the education technology industry. They explain that it is because there aren't many big news moments in this sector, and funding announcements provide a sense of timing and create opportunities to talk about larger issues. They also mention that not every funding announcement generates attention; it has to be significant enough or related to trending subjects like AI.
- Panelists further share their perspectives on how journalists are attracted towards stories that can be completed quickly, build goodwill with venture capitalists and allow them to write about other things too. Journalists tend to move on from covering funding stories after some time as they look for more meaningful topics.
- The discussion then shifts towards getting media coverage for businesses. The panel suggests thinking from an audience's perspective, offering insights instead of selling a story, starting early with local media outlets before moving onto national ones, and sharing authentic company practices or policies that resonate with people.
- Lastly, the panellist encourages start-ups considering press engagement strategies based on short-term ROI versus long-term brand building narratives. The consensus opinion suggested early-stage companies should consider if such activities energize them while using PR strategies authentically tied into their operations rather than purely product-focused pitches.
