# Firing Fast, With Confidence and Clarity

- https://share.fireflies.ai/embed/meetings/01JSSGCG213H9XXMMJ0KRRG6A7
- 0:00 Intro / Leaders in Tech Background
- 8:30 Creating a Shared Reality - The importance of clear agreements to prevent miscommunication
- 11:30 SMART Goals and Objective Setting - Set goals for clarity and objectivity. Encourages outcome-oriented performance measurement, focusing on specific, measurable deliverables. By contrast, subjective feedback affects morale and accountability.
- 16:20 Defining success and failure - and why it’s okay to celebrate them both, as long as they’re clear
- 20:28 Role-playing tough feedback conversation
- 25:50 Six-step structure for tough feedback conversations: quantifying feelings, unarguable observations, avoid judgments, discuss impact, set clear expectations, in case of failure.
- 31:18 Trust your gut your inner voice, yellow flags, and getting curious about underlying signals. Be careful and wary of your conflict avoidance bias.
- 34:00 Squash your fear of firing - replacement concerns (how long it took to find someone, how long it will take to find replacement), knowledge loss, cultural impact (team morale). Understanding the leverage that you have as an employer to encourage smoother transitions.
- 49:02 Final Thoughts: 1) No one is irreplaceable; 2) Faster vs slower transitions; 3) Fire with dignity and respect; 4) This can be a compassionate act. Don’t delay the inevitable - it’s a disservice to them and you.
