Founding AE Role
- Multi-functional early-stage role:
  - Build outbound, run demos, set up CRM
  - Give product feedback, build sales decks/process
- Avoid big-company profiles where they expect support functions you don’t have
- Need scrappy IC builders vs. pure strategists
Who to Hire / Who to Avoid
- Look for:
  - ability to sell into your ACV and segment
  - Comfort with early-stage uncertainty
  - Mid-career (5–10 years), has built basic processes before
  - Strong cultural fit and belief in the product
- Be cautious of:
  - Candidates angling for VP/CRO in 6–12 months
  - Late-career, hands-off profiles who want to hire a team too fast
  - 9–5 mindset or unrealistic equity expectations (3–5%)
Experience Profile
- Prefer strong sellers over industry insiders. Great salespeople can learn the vertical and spot patterns. Industry-specific profiles often take longer to find and may not be stronger at fundamentals.
- Match experience to motion:
  - SMB vs mid-market vs enterprise experience must map to your deal size
  - For pilot/POC-heavy motions, prior POC experience is critical
Interview & Evaluation
- Use a mock pitch of your product (45 minutes)
- Tests prep, adaptability, discovery, executive presence, brand fit
- Key signals:
  - Detailed walk-through of their biggest deal (process, not just logo)
  - Consistent quota attainment across environments
  - Curiosity and strong questions in the interview
Compensation & Structure
- Typical OTE: 50/50 base-variable; comp plan will evolve
- Equity benchmarks:
  - First AE: ~15–30 bps
  - VP Sales: ~1–2%
  - If early stage & no PMF: Higher base, little/no variable until repeatability
  - With PMF: Standard base + 3–6 month ramp/draw, higher commission for self-sourced vs founder/inbound deals
- Adjust quotas for seasonality rather than flat annual targets
Onboarding & Management
- 3–6 month ramp with clear KPIs and activity expectations
- Founder stays very involved (join early calls, run weekly pipeline reviews)
- Don’t “hire and disappear” to build product
- Expect win rates to drop from founder-led sales until AE ramps up
Outbound Benchmarks and AI Tooling
- Benchmark: 120 targeted outbound emails yield roughly 5 demos (4% conversion) when messaging and targeting are sharp.
- Email converts best overall. LinkedIn is saturated but can work with authentic content, video, and voice notes.
- Cold calling still works when backed by good data enrichment and thoughtful targeting.
- For a ~$60K ACV, a 20% win rate is very strong. Founder-led sales often outperform early AEs at first.
- Tools recommended: 
  - AI-powered: 11x ([https://www.11x.ai](https://www.11x.ai/))
  - Established: Apollo ([https://www.apollo.io](https://www.apollo.io/))
  - For data enrichment: Nooks ([https://www.nooks.in](https://www.nooks.in/)), Clay ([https://www.clay.com](https://www.clay.com/)), TitanX ([https://www.titanx.ai](https://www.titanx.ai/))
- Expect modern AEs to be fluent with AI tools, using them for research, personalization, and basic sales math modeling, not just basic prompting.
